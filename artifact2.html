Artifact 2 — Neural Network Components Diagram

Introduction: Neural networks are a core component of modern Artificial Intelligence, inspired by the structure of the human brain. This artifact provides a visual explanation of the fundamental components of neural networks, showing how data flows and how learning occurs through multiple layers and connections.

Description: This artifact is a detailed infographic illustrating the main components of a neural network, including input, hidden, and output layers, neurons, weights, biases, activation functions, loss functions, and optimization algorithms. Each element is labeled and visually connected to show its role in the learning process.

Objective: The purpose of this artifact is to help learners, students, and AI enthusiasts understand how neural networks process information, adjust weights during training, and make predictions. By visualizing the components and their interactions, complex abstract concepts are easier to grasp.

Process: To create this artifact, I conducted research on neural network structures and components, collected references from academic and online sources, and then used image generation and infographic design tools to produce a clear visual representation. I iteratively refined the layout to ensure clarity and accuracy.

Tools: ChatGPT was used to summarize technical concepts, online research provided accurate information on network components, and design tools including DALL·E and infographic software were employed to create the visual diagram.

Value Proposition: This visual guide serves as an intuitive reference for anyone learning about neural networks. It allows users to quickly understand the roles of layers, neurons, activation functions, and optimization methods, making abstract AI concepts tangible and easier to study, teach, and apply.

 

Reflection: Creating this artifact reinforced my understanding of how neural networks function. Translating technical details into a visual form helped me critically analyze the data flow, relationships between components, and the overall learning process. It also provided experience in combining research with AI-assisted tools to generate educational visuals. Overall, this exercise strengthened both my conceptual understanding of neural networks and my ability to communicate technical information effectively.
